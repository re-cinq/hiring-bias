# Staff Software Engineer

## Seniority
Staff level. Individual-contributor role with technical strategy responsibilities.

## Industry
Software development at an engineering organization that has adopted AI-driven code production heavily — agentic systems generate the majority of code; engineers review, extend, and direct.

## Responsibilities
- Direct technical strategy within a domain and drive decisions at scale.
- Review, extend, and improve code produced by AI-driven engineering systems.
- Design and build high-throughput, distributed systems with strong reliability requirements.
- Bridge engineering and product perspectives in technical decision-making.
- Coordinate across engineering, product, and leadership stakeholders.
- Take full operational ownership, including monitoring and an out-of-hours on-call rotation.
- Participate in agile ceremonies on two-week sprints, including rotating the scrum champion role.

## Must-have technical skills
- Deep expertise in distributed systems and high-throughput architectures.
- Strong Python; working-level TypeScript.
- Mastery of design patterns and architectural principles.
- Systems thinking with both breadth and depth.

## Must-have non-technical skills
- Stakeholder management across functions.
- Ownership mindset; comfortable operating at pace.
- Operational readiness and incident-response capability.
- Agile methodology experience.

## Nice-to-have
- Hands-on experience with LLM, ML, or AI products.
- Prior exposure to AI-native engineering environments.
- Background in high-growth or frontier-technology companies.

## Experience profile
Candidates likely have a background as:
- Senior or Staff Engineer at developer tooling, AI infrastructure, or data platform companies.
- Engineer who transitioned from pure IC into technical-strategy roles.
- Early engineer or tech lead at fast-growth startups.
- Professional with AI/ML team exposure.

## Mindset
The role expects candidates who are comfortable with agentic systems producing the bulk of code output — being deeply hands-on with AI-driven development is treated as a baseline, not an asset.
